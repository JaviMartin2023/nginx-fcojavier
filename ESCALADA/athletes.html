ATHLETES

Alberto

Ginés López

Alberto Ginés is a prominent Spanish climber, recognized for his remarkable achievements in competitive sport climbing. Born on February 18, 2003, in Cáceres, Spain, he displayed exceptional talent from a young age, quickly rising through the ranks in national and international competitions.

His career reached new heights when he became the first Spanish climber to win a gold medal at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, marking the debut of climbing as an Olympic sport. Ginés showcased his skill and resilience throughout the competition, overcoming world-class competitors and establishing himself as one of the top climbers of his generation.

In addition to his Olympic success, Alberto has participated in numerous World Cup events, earning multiple podium finishes. Known for his blend of technique, strength, and agility, he has tackled some of the most challenging routes worldwide. His dedication to training and passion for climbing have not only propelled his own career but also inspired a new generation of climbers.

Gold Tokio 2020 - Gold Moscú 2021 - Silver Moscú boulder 2021

Adam

Ondra

Adam Ondra is a highly accomplished Czech climber, renowned for his exceptional skills and achievements in both competition and outdoor climbing. Born on February 5, 1993, in Brno, Czech Republic, Ondra began climbing at a young age and quickly established himself as a formidable talent on the global climbing scene.

He is best known for his groundbreaking achievements in sport climbing, including being the first climber to send a route graded 9c (5.15d) with his ascent of "Silence" in Norway in 2017. Ondra has also excelled in competitions, winning multiple World Championship titles and earning gold medals at prestigious events, including the World Cup series. His versatility and technical prowess have made him a dominant figure in various climbing disciplines.

In addition to his competitive success, Ondra is an avid outdoor climber, pushing the boundaries of what is possible in the sport. His passion for climbing and commitment to exploring new routes have inspired climbers worldwide, making him a prominent figure in the climbing community and a role model for aspiring athletes.

4x World Champion - 2x Gold Moscú 2014 - Gold París 2018 - Silver Tokio 2020

Janja

Garnbret

Janja Garnbret is a highly regarded Slovenian climber, celebrated for her remarkable achievements in competition climbing. Born on March 12, 1999, in Postojna, Slovenia, she began climbing at a young age and quickly ascended to the top of the international climbing scene, showcasing her exceptional talent and dedication to the sport.

Garnbret gained global recognition for her outstanding performances in competitions, particularly in lead and bouldering disciplines. She made history at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics by winning the gold medal in women's sport climbing, becoming the first athlete to achieve this milestone in the inaugural Olympic climbing event. With multiple World Cup titles and a consistent track record of podium finishes, she is known for her technical prowess and mental strength.

Beyond her competitive success, Janja is also an accomplished outdoor climber, having completed numerous challenging routes across the globe. Her achievements and passion for climbing have made her an influential figure in the climbing community, inspiring a new generation of athletes and promoting the sport worldwide.

Gold Tokio 2020 - Gold Moscú 2014 - Gold París 2016 - Gold Hachioji 2019 - Gold 2021 Moscú - 3x World Champion

Aleksandra

Miroslaw

Aleksandra Mirosław is a prominent Polish climber known for her remarkable achievements in competition climbing, particularly in the speed climbing discipline. Born on April 1, 1997, in Warsaw, Poland, she began climbing at a young age and has since become one of the top competitors in the sport, showcasing her incredible speed and athleticism.

Mirosław gained international recognition for her exceptional performances in various competitions, including the International Federation of Sport Climbing (IFSC) World Cup circuit, where she has secured multiple gold medals. She made headlines during the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, where she won the silver medal in women's sport climbing, solidifying her status as one of the leading figures in the climbing community. Her ability to consistently perform under pressure has made her a formidable competitor.

In addition to her competition success, Aleksandra is passionate about promoting the sport and inspiring young climbers worldwide. Her dedication to training and her achievements in speed climbing have made her a role model for aspiring athletes, and she continues to push the boundaries of what is possible in the sport.

Gold Russia 2021 - Gold Munich 2021 - Gold 2022 Switzerland - 2x World Champion
